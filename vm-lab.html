Program Methodology

Phase 1 — Foundation
Policy Drafting & Stakeholder Buy-In
Drafted a formal Vulnerability Management Policy from scratch defining scope, asset classification, severity thresholds, SLA targets, and remediation ownership. Conducted mock CAB (Change Advisory Board) meetings to simulate the stakeholder alignment process — building the case for the program using business risk language, not just technical findings.
Phase 2 — Discovery
Tenable Nessus Baseline Scan on Azure VMs
Deployed Tenable Nessus against Azure-hosted Windows virtual machines. Executed a full authenticated scan to establish a vulnerability baseline. Initial scan returned 26 total findings across Critical, High, Medium, and Low severities — providing the initial risk posture and remediation target list.
Phase 3 — Remediation Round 1
Wireshark Removal
Identified Wireshark as an unauthorized and potentially exploitable application on managed endpoints. Wrote a PowerShell script to detect and silently uninstall Wireshark across target VMs. Verified removal with a targeted rescan. Findings associated with Wireshark CVEs confirmed closed.
Phase 3 — Remediation Round 2
TLS Protocol Hardening
Disabled TLS 1.0 and TLS 1.1 across all target systems — both deprecated protocols with known downgrade and BEAST attack vectors. Simultaneously disabled weak cipher suites to close associated cryptographic vulnerabilities. Applied via PowerShell registry modifications and Group Policy enforcement.
Phase 3 — Remediation Round 3
Guest Account Privilege Removal
Detected the Guest account added to the local Administrators group — a critical finding representing an unauthenticated privilege escalation path. Scripted removal of the Guest account from the Administrators group and disabled the Guest account entirely. Confirmed via rescan that the critical finding was resolved.
Phase 3 — Remediation Round 4
Windows OS Patching
Executed Windows Update patching to close outstanding OS-level CVEs — the largest single category of remaining High and Medium findings. Applied patches in a structured sequence, rebooted, and ran a final verification scan. Final posture: 6 total findings remaining, zero Criticals, 90% of High severity resolved.
Phase 4 — Reporting
Final Risk Report & Program Documentation
Produced a complete VM program report including: scan comparison before/after each round, finding severity breakdown, remediation evidence screenshots, and SLA compliance tracking. The deliverable mirrors an audit-ready enterprise VM report that a security team or compliance reviewer could evaluate directly.

Key Findings Remediated

Critical
Guest Account in Administrators Group
Unauthenticated local privilege escalation path. Guest account had been added to the local Administrators group — allowing any unauthenticated user full system control. Removed via PowerShell and confirmed closed.
High
TLS 1.0 / 1.1 Enabled
Deprecated protocols still active, exposing systems to BEAST and POODLE attacks. Disabled via registry and Group Policy. Weak cipher suites disabled concurrently to close associated CVEs.
High
Wireshark Installed — Unauthorized Tool
Network capture tool present without authorization. Associated with multiple CVEs. Silent uninstall script deployed across VMs. Post-remediation scan confirmed removal and findings closure.
High
Outstanding OS CVEs — Windows Patching Gap
Multiple unpatched Windows kernel and OS-level CVEs from delayed patch cycles. Applied Windows Update patching in a structured sequence with reboot verification. Reduced High severity count by 90%.
Medium
Weak Cipher Suite Configuration
RC4 and other deprecated cipher suites enabled alongside TLS deprecation findings. Hardened cipher suite configuration to TLS 1.2+ with strong AES-GCM suites only. Applied via PowerShell registry and IIS configuration.
Informational
Residual Findings — Risk Accepted
6 remaining findings after four remediation rounds — reviewed, classified, and formally risk-accepted per VM policy. Documented with justification, risk owner sign-off, and scheduled remediation timeline for next cycle.

Remediation Process

Phase 01 — Policy & Scope
Formal VM Policy Drafted
Wrote a formal Vulnerability Management Policy from scratch — defined scope, risk tolerance, SLA windows by severity (Critical 24hr, High 7d, Medium 30d), remediation ownership, and exception handling. Presented to mock CAB for stakeholder buy-in.
Phase 02 — Discovery
Tenable Nessus Baseline Scan
Deployed Tenable Nessus against Azure Windows VMs. Baseline scan returned 26 total findings across Critical, High, and Medium severities. Findings exported, prioritized by CVSS score and exploitability, and mapped to remediation owners.
Round 01
Wireshark Removal — Unauthorized Tool
Wireshark identified as unauthorized network capture software with associated CVEs. Silent uninstall PowerShell script deployed across all VMs. Post-remediation scan confirmed removal and finding closure across all affected systems.
Round 02
TLS 1.0 / 1.1 & Weak Cipher Suites Disabled
Deprecated TLS protocols disabled via PowerShell registry modifications and Group Policy. RC4 and other weak cipher suites replaced with TLS 1.2+ AES-GCM suite configuration. Closed BEAST and POODLE attack vectors.
Round 03
Guest Account Removed from Administrators
Critical finding: Guest account had been added to the local Administrators group — unauthenticated privilege escalation path. Removed via PowerShell and confirmed via post-scan. 100% of critical findings cleared after this round.
Round 04
Windows OS Patching — CVE Backlog
Applied Windows Update patching in structured sequence with reboot verification. Addressed outstanding kernel and OS-level CVEs from delayed patch cycles. Reduced High severity count by 90%. Remaining 6 findings formally risk-accepted with documented justification.
